05
Card design

THE CARD

Each card is an artefact. Seven fixed elements, immutable — their order and hierarchy admit no exception.

ARTIST DISPLAY RULE

The artist name appears below the song title in Cormorant Garamond italic when one of two conditions is met: the rights are licensed (partnership, direct agreement) or the work is in the public domain (classical composers: Bach, Beethoven, Orff…). In all other cases, the artist field remains empty.

POWER NAME RULE

Power names are plain Cinzel text only — no emoji, no symbols, no icons. Examples: «Iron Storm», «Riddim Flow», «Long Walk», «Resonance». The restraint of the typography carries the weight. Emoji break the card's period aesthetic.

Rarity system

THE STRATA

Six tiers. The border colour and the rarity icon in the footer both signal rarity at a glance. Each icon shape is distinct and unambiguous.

Common
Dark grey border. Base collectibles. Potentially decisive in stacks.
Uncommon

Rare

08
Brand voice

THE VOICE

The brand speaks like a battle chronicler — not a herald shouting, not a timid cleric. Every word is placed with intention.

Direct

Every sentence has one purpose. No detour, no hedging. The player immediately understands the stakes and what they can win.

Tense

There are consequences. A battle isn't played — it's won or lost. A rare card isn't found — it's earned. The tone carries pressure without hysteria.

Grounded

Music is serious here. Genres, movements, eras — treated with the weight they deserve. The epic comes from depth, not from decoration.

Write
«Build your lineup. Face the festival.»
«A rare card has appeared in the pack.»
«Ten enigmas. One answers right.»
«Stack or defeat.»
«Your MusicTree is growing. Keep going.»
Avoid
«Welcome to the magical world of music!»
«Our platform offers a unique experience.»
«Earn points and unlock rewards.»
«Please consult our terms and conditions.»
«Don't hesitate to contact us.»
09
Do's & Don'ts

THE LAWS

These rules admit no exception. Written so the brand remains recognisable across time and media.

What is right
Dark bg always #09080d — never light bg in the main interface
Gold #a87c28 reserved for value signals: score, logo, rarity, victory
Each card must carry a named power linked to the track's identity
Illustrations always original — never reproduce existing artwork
Cormorant Garamond italic for all narrative text and powers
Tone direct, grave, precise — no exclamation, no hollow enthusiasm
Type-line uses Song · [Genre] format only — no variant text
What is forbidden
Artist name only if rights are licensed or work is public domain — never by default. No album name, no lyrics
Never reproduce existing album artwork, even partially
Never mix Cinzel with another display typeface in the same heading
Never distort or recolour the logo
No corporate, administrative, or exclamatory tone in UI copy
Power names use plain text only — no emoji, no symbols. Clean Cinzel typography
Never use a light or coloured background on the main interface
