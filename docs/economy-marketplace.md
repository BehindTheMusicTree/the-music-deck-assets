# Economy and Marketplace

Defines how points enter the game, how players exchange cards, and how inflation is controlled.

## Scope

This document covers:

- marketplace mechanics
- point sources
- baseline rewards
- anti-abuse controls and daily caps

## Marketplace

- Players can exchange cards through a marketplace.
- Exchanges are priced in points to create a unified in-game economy.
- Marketplace actions can include direct buy/sell listings and player-to-player trade offers.

## How Users Gain Points

Users gain points through:

- battle rewards
- opening boosters from the active pack
- daily enigma participation rewards
- daily enigma win rewards (higher than participation)
- optional paid point bundles (freemium)

This supports both free progression and monetized progression.

## Baseline Rewards

- battle participation: 10 points
- battle win bonus: +15 points
- daily enigma participation: 2 points
- daily enigma solve bonus: +8 points
- daily enigma card unlock: 1 song card per solved enigma

## Economy Safeguards and Caps

To limit inflation and abuse:

- daily soft cap on earned points (example: 300 points/day from gameplay)
- enigma cap is naturally limited to 10 enigmas/day
- marketplace listing fee (example: 2% of listing price)
- marketplace sale tax (example: 5% of final sale)
- minimum account level before marketplace access
- optional daily trade/listing limit per account

## Pack Lifecycle and Economy Impact

- A pack represents the full card set available at a specific period.
- Booster openings are restricted to the active pack.
- New pack releases can refresh market demand and price dynamics.
- Release cadence can be seasonal (example: new pack every ~2 months).
