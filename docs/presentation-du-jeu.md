# Game Overview — The Music Deck

## Pitch (30–45 seconds)

The Music Deck is a collectible card game built around music.
Players open boosters, collect Song, Artist, and Special cards, then build lineups for tactical festival battles.
The difference: strategy is not just about rarity — it relies on genre synergies, smart stacking, and musical knowledge.
Every day, 10 enigmas let you win cards by guessing songs from AI-inspired visual clues.
The result: an experience that blends collection, pop music culture, competition, and long-term progression.

## Product Vision

Our ambition is to turn music into a strategic and collectible playground, built around:

- an accessible collection loop
- competitive depth in battle
- motivating daily progression
- a living economy via a marketplace

## Core Gameplay

## 1) Collection

- Players collect cards of three types: `Song`, `Artist`, `Special`.
- Boosters draw only from the active pack.
- A new pack can refresh the meta and the market approximately every 8 weeks.

## 2) Combat (Festival Battles)

- A battle uses 10 active slots.
- Stacking is central:
  - Same artist: 1 active slot.
  - Same album: 1 active slot.
  - Variants of the same track: stackable without consuming a new slot.
- Stack score: strongest card as the base + `15%` per additional card.
- Genre, artist, album, and special card synergies increase the score.
- Bad genre matchups reduce the score.
- Players can **save multiple decks** (named lineup configurations) and load them before a battle — for example, a deck for competitive league, another for an event or boss, a genre-focused deck for genre-ranked queues — without having to rebuild from scratch each time.

## 3) Daily Progression

- 10 enigmas per day, each tied to a track.
- The player first picks a genre, then solves the enigma.
- Solving an enigma unlocks the associated Song card.
- Missions and challenges complete the progression loop.

## 4) Long-Term: MusicTree

The `MusicTree` is a genre and subgenre tree to complete.
It pushes players to explore musical diversity, not just the most popular cards.
Progress can be measured globally, by genre, and by subgenre.

## 5) Leaderboards

Three leaderboard axes let players situate themselves and compete:

- **Global**: overall ranking by cumulative points, open to all players.
- **By country**: ranking restricted to players in the same country, for more local and accessible competition.
- **By genre / subgenre**: specialised ranking based on points earned exclusively with lineups or enigmas tied to a given genre (e.g. Rock, Hip-hop, Jazz, Progressive Metal). Rewards mastery of a musical niche rather than exhaustive collection.

Genre/subgenre leaderboards align particularly well with the `MusicTree`: a player who digs deep into a specific subgenre can dominate it without competing with generalists on the global leaderboard.

## 6) Community and Guilds (Labels)

### Labels

Players can create or join **Labels**, the guild equivalent in The Music Deck.
Each Label groups players who share a common musical identity (a genre, a scene, an era).

- A Label has a name, an identity, and a collective catalogue of covered genres.
- Members contribute individual points to the Label's global score.
- A Label can define its own internal objectives (collective enigmas, MusicTree coverage).

### Label Leaderboard

- Labels are ranked by their members' cumulative points over a given period (weekly / monthly / season).
- The ranking can be segmented by the Label's dominant genre.
- Top-ranked Labels receive **exclusive special cards** distributed to all members: Label cards, limited-edition artist cards, or thematic boosters tied to the Label's identity.

### Friends and Invitations

- Each player has a friends list showing their status (online, last battle, ranking).
- Invitations are sent by player name or via a shareable link/code.
- Between friends: direct 1v1 challenges outside standard matchmaking, with no impact on the global leaderboard (optional: friendly mode or ranked mode).
- Viewing a friend's collection, stats, and MusicTree is possible to encourage emulation and exchanges.
- A friend can be invited to join your Label directly from their profile page.

### 2v2 Battles

- Two players from the same Label (or by mutual request) can form a duo to face another duo.
- Each player builds their own 10-slot lineup; both lineups are combined to form a 20-active-slot front.
- Cross-lineup synergies are possible: same genre, same artist shared across both lineups, Label coordination bonus.
- 2v2 opens a dedicated tournament format with a separate duo leaderboard.

## 7) Achievement Badges

Badges reward notable player accomplishments and are visible on their public profile.

### Badge Categories

- **Collection**: obtain all cards for an artist, complete an album, reach N Legendary cards.
- **MusicTree**: unlock an entire genre, cover 5 distinct subgenres, explore N different genres.
- **Battle**: win 10/50/100 battles, win a 2v2, reach global top 100.
- **Enigmas**: solve 100 enigmas, chain 7 consecutive days, guess a track on the first clue.
- **Social**: invite 3 friends, co-found a Label, contribute to the top 3 of a Label leaderboard.
- **Seasonal**: badges tied to temporary events and packs, unobtainable after the season ends.

### Mechanics

- Badges have levels (Bronze / Silver / Gold) based on the threshold reached.
- Some rare badges are hidden conditions to discover.
- Earning a badge can unlock a small bonus: points, a special card, or a profile cosmetic.

## Game Economy

- Points are earned through battles, boosters, enigmas, and optional freemium progression.
- Current baselines:
  - Battle participation: `10` points
  - Win bonus: `+15`
  - Enigma participation: `2` points
  - Successful enigma bonus: `+8`
- The marketplace allows exchanges using points.
- Safeguards limit inflation (daily soft cap, listing fees, sales tax).

## Why This Game Can Stand Out

- It combines the nostalgia of card games with modern music culture.
- It rewards strategy (synergies) over simple rarity accumulation.
- It creates daily motivation through enigmas and collection objectives.
- It opens strong community opportunities: trading, musical discovery, themed events.

## Target Audience

- Collectible card game fans
- Music and pop culture fans
- Casual to competitive players who enjoy daily progression

## Short Script (90 seconds)

The Music Deck is a collectible card game inspired by the best booster games, applied to music. Players open boosters to get Song, Artist, and Special cards, then build lineups for festival battles.

The depth comes from the stack system: with 10 active slots, you must optimise which cards combine with each other. The strongest card serves as the base, and each additional card adds 15% to the stack. Genre, artist, and album synergies make the difference against opponents.

Daily, players return for 10 musical enigmas. By solving them, they earn Song cards directly, advance their missions, and grow their MusicTree — a genre and subgenre tree to complete.

The community dimension adds a strong social layer: players group into Labels, guild-like structures with a musical identity, and compete collectively for exclusive special cards. 2v2 battles open a co-operative format within Labels, with cross-lineup synergies and dedicated tournaments.

Finally, the economy ties it all together: progression points, marketplace, pack rotation, and collection dynamics. Our goal is clear: to deliver a musical game that is strategic, collectible, social, and built to last.
